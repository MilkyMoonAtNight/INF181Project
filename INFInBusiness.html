Information system integration in a business

Introduction

Information system integration in a business is the process of combining/connecting data and information from various functional areas and subsystems into a single unit. This process transforms them into a larger unified, streamlined operation that functions more efficiently with the objective to enhance efficiently and seamless data flow throughout the business organization. Information systems helps in the marketing plan helps make decisions and connect with customers.
An information system is a system with a sole purpose of possessing information. It is used in the value chain of the business to help make decisions to reach goals and objectives. Integration of the system is beneficial whether they integrate CRM (customer relationship management) or ERP (enterprise resource planning).

Benefits of system integration.

  • Automation and streamlining.

    • System integration offers multiple benefits to the business it provides streamlining and grouping of relevant data which in returns helps with automation of data across all the subsystems.

  • Connectivity.

    • Integration will also lead to improved connectivity within the business meaning all the departments (Functional areas) can communicate more clearly and efficiently share information with one another.

  • Efficiency.

    • It will potentially reduce errors that can affect the data quality as well as reduce efficiently, but with integrated system it is easier to identify errors and correct them. By limiting the error margin, it can improve efficiency of the business.

  • Information access.

    • An integrated system provides real-time data sharing and updates which allow for everyone to see up to date information that can be used in decision making and lead to faster response times and business agility.


Integration of a CRM system

The use of the Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system is a system with the main purpose of maintaining customer relationships. This system helps streamline the marketing and sales process and the organization of customer information. There are two types of CRM systems: operational and analytical.

  • Operational CRM

    • Is used when working with customers, it can be used as a transaction processing system or an information retrieval system. This system can be viewed in three levels: Contact management, Sales management, and opportunity management.

  • Analytical CRM

    • Is about the customers as a group focusing on overall information. This system can be used for Segmentation, Personalization, Response analysis, attrition analysis and Aligning supply.
