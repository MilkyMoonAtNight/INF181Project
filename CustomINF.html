Customizing information systems

Introduction

This process involves customizing software, hardware, or any other components to meet the organizations specific needs and requirements. This can either mean configuring existing software or developing new modules or interfaces for specific use. The steering committee must set priorities for the business that will identify information systems that are mandatory like systems required by legislation and information systems that provide a benefit.
There is also the option that businesses must make or buy an information system which both have benefit to the business, they can choose to make the system internally within the organization itself or outsource it by buying it of the shelf or letting another firm make custom information system for them depending on the available resources and skills in the organization.

Ensure the most out of your information system

For selecting the software, they want to obtain, they need to establish what it should do based of the following:
  1. Verifiable: stated in such a way that the requirements can be demonstrated.
  2. Clear and concise: Must be easy to read an understand, free of irrelevant information.
  3. Complete: Contains all required information.
  4. Consistent: Ensure parts do not conflict with each other.
  5. Viable: requirements can be met with the available technologies.
  6. Necessary, not optional: Must be present for the system to meet its objectives.
  7. Free of technical detail: define what mist be done without telling developers what must be done.

Developing your own information system

When developing an information systems software, they need to follow a SDLC (System development life cycle) Approach
  1. Preliminary investigation: First stage, which is determined if a proposal is feasible.
  2. System analysis: Second stage, detailed functional requirements of a system are determined.
  3. System Design: Third stage, the way a system will work is determined.
  4. System Development and test: Fourth stage, system is put into technical form and determined to operate correctly.
  5. Implementation: Fifth Stage, System is delivered to users.
  6. Operation: Sixth stage, System is used on regular basis.
  7. Maintenance: Seventh Stage, System is modified to meet new requirements.
